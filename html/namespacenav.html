Implementation of the pursuer-evader game.


The game requires two robots with two different roles: the evader and the pursuer.
The evader's objective is that of reaching one of the gates to get out of the arena.
The pursuer's objective is that of catching the evader before it is able to escape.
The evader acts unaware of the pursuer's presence. When reaching a new node of the RoadMap, it chooses one of the gates randomly and replans its path to that gate.
The pursuer is aware of the current goal the evader is heading to at every time. This is a strong assumption, but it would surely be possible to make a prediction of the gate the evader is heading to based on its short-term past motion. This implementation, however, takes the current goal for known at planning time, making it a simpler deterministic scenario.

Parameters
[in]nm_eVector of backward-computed navigation maps to all the gates in the arena.
[in]nm_pNavigation map used by the pursuer and re-computed several times throughout the game.
[in]source_eStarting pose of the evader.
[in]source_pStarting pose of the pursuer.

[out]nav_list_pOut: Navigation path of the pursuer throughout the game.
